I am a data oriented astrophysicist and cosmologist. I primarily work on methods for reliable and principled inference across large and heterogeneous datasets from next generation astronomical surveys. In particular, I have made substantial contributions to constraining generalizations of Einstein's theory of General Relativity and to imrpoving our understanding of the history and evolution of galaxies with the:

- Subaru Observatory Prime Focus Spectrograph Galaxy Evolution Survey (PFS) 
- Cosmological Advanced Survey Telescope for Optical-UV Research (CASTOR)
- Vera C. Rubin Observatory Legacy Survey of Space & Time (LSST)
- Next Generation Line Intensity Mapping Experiments

I am currently the LSST Discovery Alliance Data Science Fellowship Program Postdoctoral Scholar at Northwestern University. The DSFP is an international fellowship program intended to prepare current PhD students for data intensive science with the Vera C Rubin Observatory. I also lead the Chicago Area LSST Expansion Partnership program between Northwestern, the Illinois Institute of Technology, and the Chicago Community Colleges. This program supports community college and former transfer students in pursuing research projects related to Rubin-LSST science. If you are interested in either the LSST DA DSFP or are a Chicago area undergraduate who may be interested in the Expansion Partnership program, please feel free to reach out! 

I completed my PhD in Observational Cosmology at the University of California, Riverside in 2022. I was advised by <a href="https://sbird.github.io">Simeon Bird</a> . Before that, I was a scientist at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, California Institue of Technology where I studied Jupiter's moons, the asteroid belt, and the outer solar system. I completed my undergraduate degree at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo.
