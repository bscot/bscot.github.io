I am a data oriented astrophysicist and cosmologist. I primarily work on current and next generation large surveys. By combining data from multiple surveys we can overcome limitations inherent to each, giving us a coherent picture of the evolution of the universe across cosmic time. Current focuses include,

- Subaru Observatory Prime Focus Spectrograph Galaxy Evolution Survey (PFS) 
- Cosmological Advanced Survey Telescope for Optical-UV Research (CASTOR)
- Vera C. Rubin Observatory Legacy Survey of Space & Time (LSST)
- Line Intensity Mapping Experiments

The goal of my work is to develop methods for combining these extremely large and heterogeneous datasets in order to enable reliable and principled astrophyical and cosmological inference. 

I am currently the LSST Discovery Alliance Data Science Fellowship Program Postdoctoral Scholar at Northwestern University. The DSFP is an international fellowship program intended to prepare current PhD students for data intensive science with the Vera C Rubin Observatory. I also lead the Chicago Area LSST Expansion Partnership program between Northwestern, the Illinois Institute of Technology, and the Chicago Community Colleges. This program supports community college and former transfer students in pursuing research projects related to Rubin-LSST science. 

I completed my PhD in Observational Cosmology at the University of California, Riverside in 2022. I was advised by <a (https://sbird.github.io)='#'>Simeon Bird</a> . Before that, I was a scientist at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory, California Institue of Technology where I studied Jupiter's moons. I completed my undergraduate degree at California Polytechnic State University, San Luis Obispo.
