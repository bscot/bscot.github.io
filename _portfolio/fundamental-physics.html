The large scale structure of the universe encodes information about how the universe formed and how it has evolved over the last 13.7 billion years. From studies of the cosmic microwave background radiation, we know the universe began in a hot, dense, and uniform state. Under the influence of gravity, the universe has expanded and cooled, forming galaxies, stars, planets and people.

Although general relativity has proven an exquisite and beautiful theory for describing the evolution of structure in the universe, the observed acceleration of the expansion suggests the need for a new type of "matter" in the universe - Dark Energy. The properties of dark energy, and its history, will be encoded in how galaxies form and evolve through gravity. By studying the distribution of galaxies, we can therefore learn about dark energy or modifications to Einstein's theory of general relativity.
