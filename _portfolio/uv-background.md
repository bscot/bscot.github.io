Clustering redshift estimation is a technique for determining the distribution of a redshift dependent quantity using the cross-correlation of the quantity (say, intensity of diffuse sky emission) with a tracer catalog of objects at known redshifts. Because the error properties of clustering redshift based measurements scale mainly with the depth of the tracer catalog, this allows one to determine the redshift distribution in large photometric surveys (that cover more of the sky than spectroscopic ones) by "bootstrapping" from smaller spectroscopic surveys that can only cover a fraction of the sky area to the same depth. 

This technique is particularly useful for its role in intensity mapping experiments. Although high resolution intensity mapping measurements, in principle, have detailed knowledge of the emission redshift, this is not true in practice since large foregrounds can dominate the signal. Clustering based techniques are a promising avenue for determining the redshift dependence of an intensity mapping signal in a way that is less sensitive to cosmological uncertainties.
