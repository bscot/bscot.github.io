I am the LSST Discovery Alliance Data Science Fellowship Program Postdoctoral Scholar at the Center for Interdisciplinary Exploration and Research in Astrophysics, Northwestern University. In November 2024, I will deliver the Hunstead lecture series at the Sydney Institute of Astronomy, University of Sydney, on data science education and astrophysics.

My PhD in Physics was completed at the University of California, Riverside under the supervision of Simeon Bird. My dissertation was on measurements of fundamental physics and galaxy evolution from the largest scale structures in the universe. I am broadly interested in machine learning and statistics with applications to large astrophysical and cosmological surveys, especially the Vera C Rubin Observatory Legacy Survey of Space & Time, The Subaru Telescope Prime Focus Spectrograph Galaxy Evolution Survey, and on the design of future Line Intensity Mapping at many wavelengths. These experiments will revolutionize our understanding of fundamental physics, including the nature of dark matter and dark energy, improve our understanding of gravity on the largest scales, and the connections between galaxies and the structure of the universe.

I am also passionate about science education, social justice, and the philosophy of science. I hold certificates in higher education pedagogy, inclusive excellence, and science policy from the University of California, Riverside, and a certificate in Reflective & Effective Teaching from the Searle Center for Advancing Teaching and Learning at Northwestern University.
