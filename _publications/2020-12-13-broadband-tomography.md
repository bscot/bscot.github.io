This paper presents forecasting constraints on the amplitude and spectral energy distribution of the ultraviolet background obtained broadband tomography. Broadband tomography combines a measurement of the angular clustering of sources on the sky with an axuiliary tracer catalog of objects at known redshifts to obtain the emission distribution of a diffuse field on the sky. In this work, we show that such a measurement can potentially be improved by a factor of 2-10x with a future 1 meter class UV space telescope.
