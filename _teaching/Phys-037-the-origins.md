Discussion instructor for "The Origins", a general education course in astronomy that provides students outside of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics with an introduction to the origins and history of the universe, life, and the geophysics of the Earth. Topics include the standard model of particle physics, the concordance model of cosmology, and Darwin's theory of evolution. 

In discussion, my teaching aimed to provide students with a dedicated space for reflection on the course materials around specific topics, such as whether cosmology is a science, what distinguishes science from non-science, what it means for the universe to be "finite" or "infinite" and similar questions. In addition to reflection on the "big picture", through small group work, and practice review problems, I aimed to provide students with a basic foundation of core concepts that would provide a foundation for a lifelong interest in astronomy and cosmology.
